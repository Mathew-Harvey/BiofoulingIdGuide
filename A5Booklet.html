How to Use This Guide

The Job

You're cleaning hulls. You need to spot the bad stuff before you scrape it off. This guide shows you what to look for and what to report.

Why It Matters

Marine pests hitch rides on hulls. Some nasty ones have already turned up in WA ‚Äî like carpet sea squirt at HMAS Stirling. Divers are our first line of defence.

HIGH ‚Äî Stop work. Report immediately.
MEDIUM ‚Äî Report within 24 hours
NATIVE ‚Äî Normal WA fouling, no report needed

‚ö†Ô∏è Found Something Dodgy?

  • STOP cleaning in that area
  • Take photos ‚Äî close-up + wide shot, use hand for scale
  • Note location (bow distance, depth, port/stbd)
  • DON'T scrape it off until reported
  • Surface and tell your supervisor

HIGH
Carpet Sea Squirt
Didemnum vexillum
Sea Squirt
  • Creamy/tan like old wax
  • Veiny look ‚Äî like varicose veins
  • Leathery feel, NOT slimy
  • Drips down like candle wax
  • Already at HMAS Stirling
Don't confuse with: Native sea squirts ‚Äî thinner, gelatinous, no veiny texture
HIGH
Asian Green Mussel
Perna viridis
Mussel
  • Young = bright emerald green
  • Older = brownish-green
  • Big ‚Äî 80-165mm (hand-sized)
  • Blue-green inside when open
  • Check sea chests carefully
Don't confuse with: Blue mussel ‚Äî those are blue-black, not green

HIGH
European Fan Worm
Sabella spallanzanii
Tube Worm
  • HUGE fan ‚Äî 45-60mm across
  • Fan spirals in (not flat)
  • Stripy ‚Äî orange/purple/white bands
  • Brown leathery tube
  • Already established in WA
Don't confuse with: Native serpulid worms ‚Äî smaller fans, white hard tubes
HIGH
Black-striped False Mussel
Mytilopsis sallei
Mussel
  • Small ‚Äî only 25mm (thumbnail)
  • Dark stripes on shell
  • Packs in thick layers
  • Loves brackish water
  • Check sea chests!

White Colonial Sea Squirt
Didemnum perlucidum
Sea Squirt
  • White or see-through
  • Thin sheet ‚Äî hugs the surface
  • Smooth, not lumpy
  • Can see tiny dots inside
  • Already in WA waters

Algae

  • Feather-shaped fronds

MEDIUM Priority ‚Äî Report 24hrs

Page 1 of 3
MED
Stalked Sea Squirt
Styela clava
Sea Squirt
  • Looks like brown pickle on stalk
  • Leathery and wrinkly like prune
  • 50-160mm tall
  • Stands alone, not in colonies
MED
Brown Mussel
Perna perna
Mussel
  • Dark brown to black shell
  • Up to 90mm (fist-sized)
  • Dense thick clumps
  • Thick shell, hard to break
  • From Africa/South America

Japanese Kelp (Wakame)
Undaria pinnatifida
Kelp
  • Big brown fronds with midrib spine
  • Wavy ruffled edges
  • Can be up to 3m long
  • Frilly reproductive bit at base

Sea Squirt

  • Two holes at top (siphons)

  • Up to 150mm tall
Franmarine 8

Niche Area Hitchhikers

Check Sea Chests
NICHE
European Shore Crab
Carcinus maenas
Crab
  • Green shell (can be orange under)
  • 5 spines each side behind eyes
  • Up to 90mm across
  • Hides in sea chests & rope guards
  • Already established pest ‚Äî still report
NICHE
Asian Paddle Crab
Charybdis japonica
Crab
  • Back legs are flat paddles
  • 6 spines between the eyes
  • Up to 120mm across
  • Olive-brown colour
  • Swimming crab ‚Äî can shelter in niche areas

These don't grow on hulls but hide in sea chests, rope guards, and gratings. Check when inspecting these areas.
