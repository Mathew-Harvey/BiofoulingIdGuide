Biofouling Identification Handbook

A comprehensive guide for commercial divers working on in-water hull cleaning operations in Western Australia

Version 1.0 • December 2025

Introduction

Purpose

This handbook assists Franmarine commercial divers in identifying biofouling organisms encountered during hull cleaning operations, with emphasis on distinguishing invasive marine pests from native fouling.

Why It Matters

Early detection of invasive species is critical to protecting Australia's marine environment. Divers are often first to encounter marine pests on vessel hulls. Several high-priority pests have been detected in WA waters, including carpet sea squirt at HMAS Stirling.

Priority Levels

  • HIGH Report immediately
  • MEDIUM Report within 24 hours
  • LOW Report at end of dive

⚠️ Reporting Marine Pests

If you suspect you have found a marine pest:

  • Take clear photographs from multiple angles
  • Note location (vessel name, hull section, depth)
  • Note approximate coverage/quantity
  • DO NOT attempt to remove or relocate suspected pests
  • Report immediately to your supervisor

Part 1: Invasive Species — Priority Marine Pests

Carpet Sea Squirt
Didemnum vexillum
HIGH
Ascidian

Highly invasive colonial tunicate, also called 'sea vomit'. Forms yellowish-cream to tan mat-like colonies with visible veins. Detected at HMAS Stirling and Henderson, WA.

Key Features

  • Off-white to cream/tan/orange colour
  • Veiny appearance with visible water channels
  • Smooth, leathery texture (not slimy)
  • Can form dripping 'wax-like' tendrils
  • Small pore-like siphons on surface
Asian Green Mussel
Perna viridis
HIGH
Mussel

Large mussel (80-165mm) with distinctive bright emerald green shell. Forms dense hull and sea-chest fouling. Regularly found on vessels arriving from Asia.

Key Features

  • Bright green shell (juveniles)
  • Brownish-green (adults)
  • 80-165mm length
  • Downward-pointing beak
  • Blue-green interior
Northern Pacific Seastar
Asterias amurensis
HIGH
Seastar

Large seastar up to 50cm diameter. Voracious predator of mussels, scallops, oysters. Can release 10-25 million eggs per year. Established in Port Phillip Bay and Tasmania.

Key Features

  • Five arms with pointed upturned tips
  • Yellow/orange with purple markings
  • Up to 50cm diameter
  • Small central disc
  • Yellow underneath
European Fan Worm
Sabella spallanzanii
HIGH
Polychaete

Large tube-dwelling worm (90-400mm) with distinctive spiral crown of feeding tentacles. Established pest in WA, SA, VIC, TAS and NSW.

Key Features

  • Large spiral fan crown (45-60mm)
  • Banded orange/purple/white or grey
  • Brown leathery tube
  • Fan retracts when disturbed
  • One spiral, one circular lobe
Stalked Sea Squirt
Styela clava
MEDIUM
Ascidian

Solitary tunicate with distinctive club-shaped body on a tough stalk. Brown to tan coloured. Can grow to 160mm. Fouls hulls and aquaculture equipment.

Key Features

  • Club-shaped body on stalk
  • Brown to tan colour
  • Leathery, wrinkled texture
  • 50-160mm length
  • Solitary (not colonial)
European Shore Crab
Carcinus maenas
MEDIUM
Crab

Established marine pest with green carapace up to 90mm wide. Transported via fouling and niche areas on vessels.

Key Features

  • Green carapace (can be orange underneath)
  • Five spines each side of eyes
  • Up to 90mm carapace width
  • Found in niche areas
Japanese Kelp / Wakame
Undaria pinnatifida
MEDIUM
Macroalga

Large brown kelp with distinctive wavy fronds and midrib. Can grow to 3m. Invasive kelp established in southern Australia.

Key Features

  • Large brown fronds with midrib
  • Wavy/ruffled edges
  • Up to 3m length
  • Reproductive sporophyll at base
Vase Tunicate
Ciona intestinalis
MEDIUM
Ascidian

Common soft-bodied solitary tunicate. Translucent yellowish-green cylindrical body. Cosmopolitan fouler found worldwide.

Key Features

  • Cylindrical vase shape
  • Translucent yellowish-green
  • Two visible siphons
  • Soft body
  • Up to 150mm
Brown Mussel
Perna perna
MEDIUM
Mussel

Large brown mussel with heavy fouling potential. Native to Africa and South America. Can reach 90mm.

Key Features

  • Dark brown to black shell
  • Up to 90mm length
  • Forms dense clusters
  • Heavy hull fouler
NZ Green-lipped Mussel
Perna canaliculus
MEDIUM
Mussel

Large mussel with distinctive green lip on shell margin. Non-native to WA. Hull fouler from New Zealand vessels.

Key Features

  • Green lip along shell margin
  • Brown-green shell exterior
  • Up to 240mm length
  • Distinctive green periostracum
Black-striped False Mussel
Mytilopsis sallei
HIGH
Bivalve

Small invasive bivalve. Can tolerate low salinity. Major biofouler in tropical/subtropical ports.

Key Features

  • Small size (up to 25mm)
  • Dark stripes on shell
  • Tolerates brackish water
  • Forms dense colonies
Variable Mussel
Brachidontes pharaonis
MEDIUM
Mussel

Exotic mussel from Red Sea/Indian Ocean. Lessepsian migrant spreading via Suez Canal. Fouling pest.

Key Features

  • Variable colouration
  • Ribbed shell surface
  • Up to 35mm length
  • Intertidal/subtidal zones
Zebra Mussel
Dreissena polymorpha
HIGH
Bivalve

Freshwater pest with potential to foul internal systems. Distinctive zebra-striped pattern. Major infrastructure fouler.

Key Features

  • Zebra-like striped pattern
  • D-shaped shell
  • Up to 50mm length
  • Attaches by byssal threads
Quagga Mussel
Dreissena bugensis
HIGH
Bivalve

Freshwater internal system fouler. Similar to zebra mussel but rounder shell. Clogs pipes and infrastructure.

Key Features

  • Rounder than zebra mussel
  • Pale bands/stripes
  • Ventral side convex
  • Up to 40mm length
Basket Clam
Varicorbula gibba
MEDIUM
Bivalve

Introduced clam found in sediments. Opportunistic coloniser in disturbed environments.

Key Features

  • Small white shells
  • Up to 12mm length
  • Asymmetric valves
  • Lives in soft sediment
Pacific Oyster
Magallana gigas
MEDIUM
Bivalve

Large oyster that colonises hard structures. Establishes reef-like formations. Significant structure fouler.

Key Features

  • Large irregular shell
  • Up to 400mm length
  • Rough fluted surface
  • Cements to hard surfaces
White Colonial Sea Squirt
Didemnum perlucidum
HIGH
Ascidian

Invasive colonial tunicate found in WA. Forms thin translucent white colonies on hulls and infrastructure.

Key Features

  • White/translucent colonies
  • Thin encrusting sheets
  • Small zooids visible
  • Smooth surface texture
Pleated Sea Squirt
Styela plicata
MEDIUM
Ascidian

Cosmopolitan solitary tunicate. Common hull fouler worldwide. Distinct pleated/ridged body surface.

Key Features

  • Pleated/ridged surface
  • Grey-brown colour
  • Up to 70mm height
  • Solitary, not colonial
Chinese Mitten Crab
Eriocheir sinensis
HIGH
Crab

Exotic species that can be transported in niche areas. Distinctive furry claws. Catadromous lifecycle.

Key Features

  • Dense fur on claws ("mittens")
  • Olive-brown carapace
  • Up to 80mm carapace width
  • Notched forehead
Harris Mud Crab
Rhithropanopeus harrisii
HIGH
Crab

Priority pest crab. Small mud crab that tolerates low salinity. Found in estuarine environments.

Key Features

  • Small size (up to 25mm)
  • Olive-brown colour
  • White-tipped claws
  • Three spines behind eyes
Asian Paddle Crab
Charybdis japonica
MEDIUM
Crab

Non-native swimming crab. Paddle-like rear legs for swimming. Can shelter in vessel niche areas.

Key Features

  • Paddle-shaped rear legs
  • Six spines between eyes
  • Up to 120mm carapace
  • Olive to brown colour
Brackish Hydroid
Blackfordia virginica
MEDIUM
Hydroid

Introduced hydroid/jellyfish found in estuarine waters. Medusa stage is small bell-shaped jellyfish.

Key Features

  • Small medusa (up to 22mm)
  • Transparent bell
  • Polyp stage on fouling
  • Tolerates brackish water
Comb Jelly
Mnemiopsis leidyi
HIGH
Comb Jelly

Ballast water pest. Gelatinous predator that devastated Black Sea fisheries. Spreads via ballast water.

Key Features

  • Transparent gelatinous body
  • Eight rows of cilia (combs)
  • Up to 120mm length
  • Iridescent in light
Feather Algae / Killer Algae
Caulerpa taxifolia
HIGH
Macroalga

Invasive strain of green algae. Spreads rapidly smothering native species. Can attach to anchor chains.

Key Features

  • Bright green feather-like fronds
  • Up to 65cm height
  • Creeping stolons
  • Dense meadow formation
Hook Weed
Bonnemaisonia hamifera
MEDIUM
Macroalga

Introduced red algae with distinctive hooked branches. Spreads via fragments attaching to fouling.

Key Features

  • Red/pink branching fronds
  • Hook-like branchlets
  • Up to 30cm height
  • Pompom-like stage (Trailliella)

Part 2: Native Species — Common Fouling Organisms

Acorn Barnacles
Balanidae family
Barnacle

Common native barnacles found on hulls, propellers and sea chests. Conical white/grey shells cemented to surfaces.

Key Features

  • Conical white/grey shells
  • Diamond-shaped opening
  • Cement directly to surfaces
  • Dense clusters
Sydney Coral / Tube Worm
Galeolaria caespitosa
Polychaete

Native tube-forming worm that creates white calcareous tube colonies. Forms distinctive reef-like masses.

Key Features

  • White calcareous tubes
  • Colonial masses
  • Reef-like formations
  • Pink feeding tentacles
Bushy Bryozoans
Bugula spp.
Bryozoan

Native bushy/branching bryozoan colonies. Purple to brown coloured with delicate branching structure.

Key Features

  • Bushy branching growth
  • Purple/brown colour
  • Delicate feathery appearance
  • Flexible branches
Blue Mussel
Mytilus galloprovincialis
Mussel

Common native structure fouler. Blue-black to dark brown shell. Forms dense clusters attached by byssal threads.

Key Features

  • Blue-black to dark brown shell
  • Elongated triangular shape
  • Dense clustering
  • Byssal thread attachment
Sea Lettuce
Ulva australis
Algae

Common native green algae. Bright green translucent sheets with ruffled edges. Often found in nutrient-rich waters.

Key Features

  • Bright green colour
  • Thin translucent sheets
  • Ruffled/wavy edges
  • Soft texture
Encrusting Sponges
Demospongiae
Sponge

Various native encrusting sponges forming sheets or cushion-like growths. Variable colours including orange, yellow, grey.

Key Features

  • Sheet or cushion growth
  • Variable colours
  • Soft spongy texture
  • Small pores visible
Large Red Barnacles
Megabalanus spp.
Barnacle

Large conical barnacles with distinctive red/pink colouration. Common on vessel hulls and hard substrates.

Key Features

  • Large conical shells
  • Red/pink colouration
  • Up to 50mm diameter
  • Ribbed shell plates
Volcano Barnacles
Tetraclita spp.
Barnacle

Distinctive pink/green volcano-shaped barnacles. Common intertidal species on rocky shores and hulls.

Key Features

  • Pink/green colouration
  • Volcano-like shape
  • Four shell plates
  • Up to 40mm diameter
Serpulid Worms
Serpulidae & Spirorbidae
Polychaete

Tube-dwelling worms that build white calcareous tubes. Common foulers on all hard substrates.

Key Features

  • White calcareous tubes
  • Spirorbids are coiled (3mm)
  • Serpulids larger, straight/curved
  • Feathery feeding crown
Red-rust Bryozoan
Watersipora spp.
Bryozoan

Sheet-forming encrusting bryozoan. Distinctive red/rust/orange colouration. Very common hull fouler.

Key Features

  • Red/rust/orange sheets
  • Encrusting growth form
  • Calcified zooids
  • Black growing edge
Chalky Bryozoans
Celleporaria spp.
Bryozoan

White/pink encrusting bryozoans forming chalky crusts on hard substrates. Common on hulls.

Key Features

  • White/pink crusts
  • Chalky texture
  • Irregular lumpy growth
  • Hard calcified surface
Solitary Sea Squirts
Cnemidocarpa spp.
Ascidian

Native solitary tunicates with orange/white colouration. Rounded to oval body shape.

Key Features

  • Orange/white body
  • Solitary individuals
  • Two visible siphons
  • Leathery tunic
Colonial Sea Squirts (Native)
Didemnidae (native)
Ascidian

Native colonial tunicates forming thin gelatinous mats. Compare carefully with invasive Didemnum species.

Key Features

  • Thin gelatinous mats
  • Variable colours
  • Small zooids in colony
  • Shared cloacal openings
Hairy Mussel
Trichomya hirsuta
Mussel

Native mussel with distinctive hairy periostracum. Forms dense clumps on subtidal substrates.

Key Features

  • Hairy shell covering
  • Brown/black shell
  • Dense cluster formation
  • Up to 60mm length
Small Black Mussel
Septifer bilocularis
Mussel

Small native mussel that attaches to hard substrates. Black shell with ribbed surface.

Key Features

  • Small size (up to 40mm)
  • Black/dark brown shell
  • Ribbed shell surface
  • Septum inside shell
Green Hair Algae
Cladophora spp.
Algae

Filamentous green algae forming mats on fouled surfaces. Common in nutrient-rich waters.

Key Features

  • Bright green filaments
  • Branching structure
  • Forms tangled mats
  • Coarse texture
Brown Filamentous Algae
Hincksia spp.
Algae

Fine brown filamentous algae. Early coloniser on new fouling. Forms fuzzy coating.

Key Features

  • Fine brown filaments
  • Fuzzy appearance
  • Early coloniser
  • Soft delicate texture
Juvenile Sargassum
Sargassum spp.
Algae

Brown algae with small bladders. Juveniles attach to hard substrates including hulls. Small brown tufts.

Key Features

  • Brown branching fronds
  • Small air bladders
  • Leaf-like structures
  • Holdfast attachment
Red Algae
Gracilaria & Gelidium spp.
Algae

Common red algae found on hulls and infrastructure. Cartilaginous texture with branching structure.

Key Features

  • Red/purple colour
  • Branching fronds
  • Cartilaginous texture
  • Common in subtidal zone
Amphipods
Amphipoda
Crustacea

Small mobile crustaceans found in fouling communities. Important detritivores and prey species.

Key Features

  • Small size (1-30mm)
  • Laterally compressed body
  • Multiple leg pairs
  • Fast swimmers/crawlers
Marine Isopods
Isopoda
Crustacea

Flat slater-like crustaceans. Found crawling in fouling communities. Some bore into wood.

Key Features

  • Dorsoventrally flattened
  • Seven pairs of legs
  • Segmented body
  • Variable sizes (2-50mm)
Small Crabs
Pachygrapsus spp.
Crab

Small native shore crabs that hide in fouling communities. Square carapace with striped legs.

Key Features

  • Square-ish carapace
  • Striped legs
  • Up to 50mm width
  • Fast moving
Small Seastars
Patiriella spp.
Echinoderm

Small native cushion stars. Occasional visitors to fouled surfaces. Various colours.

Key Features

  • Small size (up to 40mm)
  • Five short arms
  • Variable colours
  • Cushion-like shape
Anemones / Chitons / Limpets
Multiple taxa
Various

Miscellaneous native fouling fauna including anemones, chitons, and limpets found on hull surfaces.

Key Features

  • Anemones: soft-bodied, tentacles
  • Chitons: 8-plate shell
  • Limpets: conical shell
  • Native fouling community
